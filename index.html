A solar eclipse occurs when the moon passes in front of the sun, blocking it out partially or completely. The eclipse results in parts of the earth being covered in the shadow of the moon. Eventually the moon will be much farther away from Earth and will appear smaller.

In a lunar eclipse, the Moon moves into the shadow of Earth cast by the Sun. This last type of eclipse happens when the Moon is farthest in its orbit from Earth and Earth is closest in its orbit to the Sun, which makes the Moon's disk too small to cover the Sun's disk completely.
